## Water Damage Restoration FAQ

**1. How fast should I respond to water damage in Mount Hood Village, OR?**

Immediate action is critical. Water can cause structural damage within hours and mold within one to two days. Calling a professional restoration service right away prevents small problems from becoming major repairs.

**2. What causes most water damage in Mount Hood Village homes?**

Common causes include roof leaks from heavy rain or snow, frozen pipes, appliance failures, and basement flooding from poor drainage during storms or snowmelt.

**3. Can I dry out water damage myself?**

Small spills may be manageable, but hidden moisture in walls and floors requires professional equipment. Without proper drying, mold and structural damage are likely to develop.

**4. How long does water damage restoration take?**

Most projects take several days to a week depending on severity. Drying alone can take 2–4 days, followed by repairs and cleaning.

**5. Is water damage restoration covered by insurance?**

Coverage depends on the cause of the damage. Sudden and accidental events are often covered, while long-term leaks may not be.

**6. What should I do first after discovering water damage?**

Shut off the water source if possible and call a professional restoration service immediately. Avoid using electrical appliances in wet areas.

**7. Will mold grow after water damage?**

Mold can grow quickly if moisture is not removed properly. Professional drying and sanitizing significantly reduce this risk.

**8. Can furniture and belongings be saved?**

Many items can be restored if treated quickly. Restoration professionals evaluate what can be cleaned and dried safely.

**9. Is storm damage restoration different from regular water damage cleanup?**

Yes. Storm damage often includes debris, roof leaks, and large-scale flooding that requires more extensive extraction and drying.
